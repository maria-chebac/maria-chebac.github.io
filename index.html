Work

Political Representation of Women in Romania - March '23 Challenge of RomanianDATA Trybe on Linkedin

The findings from the study suggested by RomanianDATA Tribe confirmed my suspicions: true gender equality in Romanian politics remains elusive. The lack of women running for prominent political positions is still prevalent, and the proportion of those who are successfully elected is even smaller.

Conclusions of the study:
▶ To effectively promote gender equality, it is essential to conduct a thorough review of the existing legislative framework.

▶This review should aim to identify any gaps and weaknesses in the system that may hinder the progress of gender equality. In addition, it is imperative to ensure transparency in all procedures and information related to elections, including the accurate collection and management of data on gender, age, and occupation of candidates and elected officials.

▶Furthermore, efforts should be made to create more opportunities for women to participate in politics by organizing gender equality programs in schools, women organizations, forums, and conferences. By doing so, we can expand the interest and involvement of women in running for elected positions, ultimately leading to a more balanced representation of genders in politics.
